Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

This interactive scatterplot chart contains data on rates of income, obesity, poverty, etc. by state in the U.S. The data set included is based on 2014 ACS 1-year estimates from the Census Bureau and the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System. Click through each variable combination to see how they relate!

Overall trends:

  • The state with the highest rate of lack of healthcare is Texas with almost 25%, whereas Massachusetts is the lowest with 4.6%.
  • The poorest state corresponds to Mississippi with over 21% of poverty rate and lowest median household income below $40,000.
  • Smoking and obesity rates present similar patterns with West Virginia and Arkansas presenting some of the highest rates.

Correlations observed between variables:

  • Lack of healthcare positively correlates with poverty, does not correlate with age and negatively correlates with income.
  • Similarly, smoking positively correlates with poverty, does not correlate with age and negatively correlates with income.
  • Similarly, obesity positively correlates with poverty, does not correlate with age and negatively correlates with income.
